Welcome

Large Language Models (LLMs) and multi-agent systems are transforming recommendation systems by enabling more personalized, contextual, and interactive user experiences. This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to explore how these technologies are reshaping the future of recommendations.

Topics of Interest

  • LLM-based recommendation systems
  • Multi-agent architectures for recommendations
  • Conversational and interactive recommendation systems
  • Personalization using foundation models
  • Trust, safety, and ethics in agent-based systems
  • Evaluation metrics for LLM-powered recommendations

Workshop Overview

The rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) and multi-agent systems presents unprecedented opportunities to revolutionize recommendation systems. This workshop explores cutting-edge research at the intersection of these technologies, focusing on how LLMs and agents can create more intelligent, context-aware, and personalized recommendation experiences.

We invite submissions on novel architectures, evaluation methodologies, real-world deployments, and the societal implications of these powerful new approaches to recommendations.

Overview

We invite submissions that explore the intersection of Large Language Models, multi-agent systems, and recommendation systems. We welcome both research papers and position papers that advance our understanding of how these technologies can work together to create better recommendation experiences.

Topics of Interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

  • LLM-powered Recommendations: Novel architectures and approaches using foundation models
  • Multi-Agent Systems: Coordination, communication, and collaboration among recommendation agents
  • Conversational Recommendations: Interactive and dialogue-based recommendation systems
  • Personalization: User modeling and adaptation using LLMs and agents
  • Evaluation: Metrics and methodologies for assessing LLM/agent-based recommendations
  • Trust & Safety: Fairness, transparency, and accountability in agent-based systems
  • Real-world Deployments: Case studies and lessons learned from production systems
  • Human-AI Interaction: User experience and interface design for agent-based recommendations

Submission Guidelines

Format: Papers should follow the ACM format (use the ACM Primary Article Template)

Length:

  • Full papers: 6-8 pages (excluding references)
  • Short papers: 4-5 pages (excluding references)
  • Position papers: 2-3 pages (excluding references)

Review Process: All submissions will be peer-reviewed by the program committee. Reviews will be single-blind.
