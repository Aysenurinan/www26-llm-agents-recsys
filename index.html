LLM & Agents for Recommendation Systems

The Web Conference 2026 (WWW'26) • Dubai, UAE

Abstract

Recommendation systems underpin much of the web economy—from content feeds and e-commerce to advertising and social interaction. As Large Language Models (LLMs) and autonomous agents increasingly power these systems, we are witnessing a shift from centralized recommendation pipelines to agentic ecosystems that plan, negotiate, and interact across product discovery, personalization, and fulfillment.

LLMs introduce new capabilities for reasoning over multi-modal data, grounding recommendations in natural language, and enabling conversational, zero-shot personalization. Meanwhile, multi-agent systems unlock emergent coordination across buyers, sellers, and platforms—yet raise challenges of interpretability, robustness, and evaluation. Traditional short-term metrics like clicks and conversions fail to capture coordination quality, long-horizon trust, or fairness across stakeholders.

This workshop convenes the communities of recommendation systems, multi-agent learning, information retrieval, and mechanism design to define principles for transparent, scalable, and responsible agent-driven personalization. We invite work on architectures for LLM-integrated recommendation, agent collaboration, retrieval-augmented generation, reasoning and alignment, evaluation frameworks, fairness and trust, and real-world deployment case studies. By bridging academic insights with industry deployments, this forum seeks to shape the next generation of LLM and agent-based recommendation systems—systems that are adaptive, explainable, and aligned with user and societal welfare.

Call for Papers

We invite submissions exploring the intersection of Large Language Models (LLMs), intelligent agents, and recommendation systems. This workshop aims to advance the understanding of how LLM-based agents can enhance personalization, user interaction, and decision-making in recommendation systems.

Topics of Interest

We welcome research contributions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • LLM-based Recommendation Agents: Design, architecture, and deployment of intelligent recommendation agents
  • Multi-Agent Systems: Coordination, collaboration, and optimization in multi-agent recommendation environments
  • Conversational & Interactive Recommendations: Natural language interfaces, dialogue systems, and user engagement strategies
  • Evaluation & Benchmarking: Novel metrics, datasets, and evaluation frameworks for LLM-based recommenders
  • Personalization & Context-Awareness: Leveraging user context, preferences, and historical data for enhanced personalization
  • Explainability & Transparency: Interpretability of LLM-driven recommendations and building user trust
  • Real-World Applications: Case studies and deployments in e-commerce, content platforms, healthcare, finance, and more
  • Ethical & Societal Implications: Bias, fairness, privacy, and responsible AI in recommendation systems
  • Hybrid Approaches: Combining LLMs with traditional recommender techniques (collaborative filtering, matrix factorization, etc.)
  • Knowledge Graphs & Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Enhancing recommendations with structured knowledge and retrieval mechanisms
